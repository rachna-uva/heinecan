Emissions: From Fossil Fuels and Land Use Change

GtC/year: Gigatons of carbon per year

Data sourced from The Global Carbon Project: Friedlingstein et al. 2022b

The concentration of carbon dioxide (CO2) in the atmosphere has increased since the beginning of the industrial era. Initially, it was mostly due to deforestation and other land use change activities. Fossil fuels became the main source of emissions from around 1950, and their relative share has continued to increase since then. These additional, human-caused emissions are on top of the natural carbon cycle on Earth, which circulates carbon between the atmosphere, ocean and land.
