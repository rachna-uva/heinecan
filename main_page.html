Carbon Budget: Imbalanced

GtC/year: Gigatons of carbon per year

Data sourced from The Global Carbon Project: Friedlingstein et al. 2022b

The carbon budget imbalance is the sum of emissions minus the sum of sinks. It is a measure of scientists' understanding of humans on the carbon cycle. Our budget is decreasing over the decades, i.e., we are heading towards the Earth being able to absorb antopogenic emissions less and less.

Accurately assessing the the anthopogenic contribution to global carbon emissions and their distribution in the atmosphere, ocean and land in the anthropocene is important to understand. This can help develop climate policies and improve climate change projection models. Balancing the carbon budget is essential to adapting to the reality of climate change.
