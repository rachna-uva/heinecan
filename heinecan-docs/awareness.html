Heat Stress Awareness

Understanding Heat Stress at Work

Heat stress occurs when the body cannot cool itself adequately during exposure to high temperatures. In industrial environments like breweries, logistics centers, or outdoor work zones, this risk is intensified. This is especially during summer or in tropical regions.

What Is Heat Stress?

Heat stress is the body’s response to excessive heat. It can cause physical exhaustion, dehydration, and even life-threatening conditions like heatstroke. The Universal Thermal Climate Index (UTCI) is used to assess this risk by combining temperature, humidity, and wind speed.

Risk Levels Explained

Risk Level Meaning
No RiskConditions are safe. No special measures needed.
Moderate RiskDiscomfort may occur. Stay hydrated.
Strong RiskTake precautions. Use shade and rest periods.
Very Strong RiskHigh chance of heat stress. Apply full mitigation steps.
Extreme RiskDangerous conditions. Avoid physical activity. Activate emergency protocol.

How to Stay Safe

  • Hydration: Drink one cup of water (250 ml) every 15 to 20 minutes. It is not safe to consume more than 6 cups (about 1.5 liters) per hour.
  • Cooling: Take breaks in shaded/cool areas, and use fans or cool packs.
  • Clothing: Wear lightweight, breathable clothing.
  • Monitoring: Use the HSMD dashboard to monitor conditions and follow mitigation guidelines.

Common Symptoms to Watch For

Condition Symptoms Severity Treatment
Heat Rash Red clusters of pimples or small blisters (neck, chest, groin) Mild discomfort Keep affected area dry and cool; use mild drying lotions and clean skin to prevent infection; avoid tight clothing
Heat Cramps Painful muscle spasms (legs, arms, abdomen), often after heavy sweating Moderate Rest in a cool area, drink water or sports drink, gently stretch affected muscles
Heat Exhaustion Heavy sweating, dizziness, fatigue, nausea, clammy skin, rapid heartbeat Serious – requires rest and rehydration Move to shaded area/AC, hydrate with cool drinks, apply wet towels, rest until fully recovered or send home
Heat Stroke Confusion, unconsciousness, hot/dry skin, seizures, body temp > 40°C Medical emergency – immediate response needed Call for medical emergency, move to cool area, remove clothing, use fans/iced water to cool aggressively

Workers experiencing symptoms must immediately stop work and notify a supervisor. A delay in response can escalate symptoms rapidly.
